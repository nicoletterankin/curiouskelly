# Lesson Authoring Guide - Curious Kellly

## üéØ **The Universal Lesson Concept**

Every day, **everyone learns the same topic**, but Kelly adapts her age, language, and examples to match the learner's age (2-102 years old).

**Example:**
- **Topic:** "Why Do Leaves Change Color?"
- **2-year-old sees:** 3-year-old Kelly saying "Leaves are like blankets! They change colors when it gets cold!"
- **35-year-old sees:** 27-year-old Kelly explaining chlorophyll breakdown and anthocyanin production
- **82-year-old sees:** 82-year-old Kelly reflecting on seasons of life and nature's cycles

**Everyone learns about leaves together, but in age-appropriate ways.**

## üìã **Topic Selection Criteria**

### ‚úÖ **Good Universal Topics:**
1. **Observable phenomena** - Leaves, clouds, shadows, reflections
2. **Universal experiences** - Friendship, curiosity, gratitude, patience
3. **Natural wonders** - Rain, rainbows, stars, seasons
4. **Human behaviors** - Laughter, helping, sharing, listening
5. **Everyday mysteries** - Why is the sky blue? How do birds fly? What is time?

### ‚ùå **Avoid:**
1. **Age-specific content** - Retirement planning, dating, homework
2. **Controversial topics** - Politics, religion (unless universal spiritual concepts)
3. **Commercial products** - Brand names, products
4. **Culturally specific** - Holidays, traditions (unless explained universally)
5. **Overly technical** - Advanced math, specialized skills

## üìù **Writing for Each Age Group**

### **Ages 2-5: Toddler-Friend Kelly (age 3)**

**Voice:** Playful, simple, concrete

**Language:**
- Very short sentences (3-7 words)
- Concrete nouns, simple verbs
- Repetition is good
- Use sound words (splash, whoosh, boom)

**Examples:**
```
"Look! The leaf is red now!"
"It was green. Now it's red!"
"Trees get ready for sleep."
```

**Interactions:**
- Show me
- Point to
- Let's pretend
- Can you...?

**Pacing:** 30s welcome, 2min teaching, 1min practice, 30s reflection, 30s wisdom
**Total:** ~5 minutes

---

### **Ages 6-12: Curious-Kid Kelly (age 9)**

**Voice:** Enthusiastic, wondering, encouraging

**Language:**
- Short to medium sentences
- "Why" and "how" questions
- Relate to their world (school, friends, pets)
- Still concrete but introducing abstract

**Examples:**
```
"Have you ever wondered why leaves change color in fall?"
"It's like the tree is getting ready for a long nap!"
"The green stuff in leaves is called chlorophyll..."
```

**Interactions:**
- What do you think?
- Have you noticed...?
- Let's investigate
- Try this experiment

**Pacing:** 30s welcome, 3min teaching, 1.5min practice, 1min reflection, 30s wisdom
**Total:** ~6.5 minutes

---

### **Ages 13-17: Teen-Mentor Kelly (age 15)**

**Voice:** Real, relatable, thoughtful

**Language:**
- Natural teen voice (but not slang)
- Acknowledge complexity
- Connect to identity and values
- Ask open-ended questions

**Examples:**
```
"So leaves changing color isn't just random‚Äîthere's actual chemistry happening."
"Think about it: trees have this whole system for surviving winter."
"Kind of like how we adapt to challenges, right?"
```

**Interactions:**
- What's your take?
- How does this connect to...?
- Consider this perspective
- What if...?

**Pacing:** 30s welcome, 3.5min teaching, 2min practice, 1.5min reflection, 1min wisdom
**Total:** ~8.5 minutes

---

### **Ages 18-35: Knowledgeable-Adult Kelly (age 27)**

**Voice:** Informed, practical, engaging

**Language:**
- Sophisticated vocabulary
- Evidence-based explanations
- Real-world applications
- Nuanced thinking

**Examples:**
```
"The autumn color change is driven by decreasing daylight and temperature."
"Chlorophyll breaks down, revealing carotenoids and anthocyanins."
"This process has practical implications for climate research..."
```

**Interactions:**
- In your experience...?
- Consider the implications
- How might this apply to...?
- Reflect on...

**Pacing:** 30s welcome, 4min teaching, 2.5min practice, 2min reflection, 1min wisdom
**Total:** ~10 minutes

---

### **Ages 36-60: Experienced-Guide Kelly (age 48)**

**Voice:** Wise, balanced, perspective-rich

- Mature vocabulary
- Long-term thinking
- Life lessons and wisdom

**Examples:**


**Interactions:**
- Looking back, how has...?
- What wisdom would you share?
- In your experience...
- How has your understanding evolved?

**Pacing:** 1min welcome, 4min teaching, 2.5min practice, 2.5min reflection, 1.5min wisdom
**Total:** ~11.5 minutes

## üõ†Ô∏è **Step-by-Step Authoring Process**

### **Step 1: Choose Your Topic** (15 min)
- Pick from curriculum list or create new
- Ensure it's universal (works for ages 2-102)
- Verify it's age-adaptable
- Check it's safe and appropriate

### **Step 2: Core Concept** (30 min)
- Write the universal truth/concept in one sentence
- Example: "Leaves change color because trees prepare for winter by breaking down chlorophyll"
- Ensure this concept can be explained at all age levels

### **Step 3: Age-Specific Content** (2-3 hours per age group)
- Start with ages 18-35 (easiest baseline)
- Then simplify for younger ages (2-5, 6-12, 13-17)
- Then add depth for older ages (36-60, 61-102)

**For each age group, write:**
1. **Welcome** (30s-1min) - Hook them in
2. **Main Content** (2-4.5min) - Core teaching
3. **Key Points** (3-5 bullet points) - Main takeaways
4. **Interaction Prompts** (3-5 questions) - Engage learner
5. **Wisdom Moment** (30s-2min) - Memorable insight

### **Step 4: Teaching Moments** (30 min per age group)
- Add 2-3 interactive moments
- Types: visual, inquiry, reflection, application, wisdom, legacy
- Include timing (when in lesson)

### **Step 5: Validate** (15 min)
```bash
node curious-kellly/content-tools/validate-lesson.js your-lesson.json
```

### **Step 6: Generate Audio** (1 hour per age group)
```bash
node curious-kellly/content-tools/generate-audio.js your-lesson.json --age-group 18-35
```

### **Step 7: Preview** (5-10 min)
```bash
node curious-kellly/content-tools/preview-lesson.js your-lesson.json --age 35
```

### **Step 8: Test** (15 min)
- Load in backend
- Test via API
- Verify all age variants work

**Total time per lesson:** ~12-15 hours (with 6 age variants)

## üí° **Pro Tips**

### **Efficiency Hacks:**
1. **Batch similar ages:** Write 2-5 and 6-12 together (simplification)
2. **Use voice recorder:** Speak Kelly's voice, then transcribe
3. **Test with real people:** Get feedback from each age group
4. **Reuse structure:** Copy pacing/format from "Leaves"

### **Common Pitfalls:**
1. ‚ùå Making topic too narrow (e.g., "401k planning")
2. ‚ùå Using same language for all ages
3. ‚ùå Forgetting to update Kelly's age
4. ‚ùå Making lessons too long (respect attention spans)
5. ‚ùå Not testing audio flow

### **Content Sources:**
- Scientific databases (NIH, NASA, etc.)
- Educational research
- Your own life experience
- Classic literature and wisdom
- Nature observation
